Hi,

I'm Charlotte. I'm a UX architect with a focus on design and cartography. In my free time, I enjoy contributing to OpenStreetMap.

You can examples of my work in my design portfolio, or on Bēhance and Dribbble. I post photos on Unsplash, Flickr, and Instagram. I keep random thoughts on Twitter and Medium. I have references and cool ideas on Pinterest and Tumblr.

Most of my code belongs to my employers, but I post some snippets up on GitHub, Bitbucket and CodePen.

Work Experience

Presently, my work is focused on a combination of geospatial data visualization, user experience research and interface design for the Albuquerque resiliency consulting firm RS21. However, my background is in art/design, 3D modeling and environmental research.

You can find my full CV on LinkedIn.

Graduate School

I received my Master of Architecture degree from the University of New Mexico SA+P in 2017. I graduated with distinction, and was awarded the ARCC King Student Medal for Excellence in Architectural and Environmental Design Research.

While at UNM, I had the opportunity to research the intersection between digital and physical spaces, to learn about photogrammetry and other cutting-edge digital capture techniques, and to work as a web developer. In addition, as a Graduate Research Assistant, I designed a visualization tool for the National Park Service Cultural Landscapes Inventory at Bandelier National Monument.

Undergraduate School

I have a B.A. in Environmental Studies and Communication/Media Studies from Franklin University in Lugano, Switzerland. I graduated magna cum laude in 2013.

I had the opportunity to participate in Franklin's Academic Travel program, where I studied culture, finance, and history across Europe. I also co-created Franklin's Travel Ecological Footprint Calculator web application alongside my friend, Daniel Merkle.

Travel

Travel has always been a passion of mine. In high school, I was fortunate to have the opportunity to visit Tokyo and Kyoto, and later attended school in in Kawasaki, Japan. I have snapshots of my time as a study abroad posted on Flickr.
